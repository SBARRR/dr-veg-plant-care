Sago Palm

Today, let the soil dry out a bit more before watering again since it recently received rain and the soil was still moist a few days ago. Keep an eye on older yellowing fronds but leave them alone unless they become mostly brown or loose. Enjoy the warm weather and the filtered afternoon sun your sago palm is getting!

Top priority/action for today: Check the soil moisture about 1–2 inches deep and only water if it feels dry.

Watering guidance: Water thoroughly until drainage, then let excess water drain away; never let the pot sit in standing water.

One useful check: Gently tug any yellowing fronds to see if they are loosening, but don’t remove unless they pull away easily or are mostly brown.

Optional care improvement: If you haven’t already, clear away the decorative top dressing around the soil surface to better monitor moisture and avoid hidden sogginess.

Optional practical idea: Place a small fan nearby on low for better airflow after rain or watering to reduce rot risk.
