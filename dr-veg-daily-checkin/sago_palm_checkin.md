Sago Palm

Today, let the soil dry out a bit more before watering again to avoid soggy roots in that shallow bonsai pot. Keep an eye on the older yellowing fronds but don’t remove them yet unless they turn fully brown or pull away easily. The warm, humid weather means the plant will use water steadily but still needs good drainage and airflow.

Top priority/action for today: Check the top 1–2 inches of soil moisture and only water if it feels dry and the pot is noticeably lighter.

Watering guidance: Water thoroughly until it drains, then empty any collected water from the drip tray to prevent standing water.

One useful check: Inspect the undersides of fronds and the crown for any tiny scale or pest signs, especially since spotting was seen before.

Optional care improvement: If you want, gently lift the top dressing to check that the soil below is not staying too wet or compacted.

Optional practical idea: Rotate the pot slightly to ensure even light exposure on all sides of the plant.
