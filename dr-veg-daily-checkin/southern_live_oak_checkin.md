Your Southern Live Oak is settling into its new outdoor spot, showing resilience after the recent transplant. The increased sun exposure should help its recovery, but the recent heat means we need to watch for signs of stress like leaf scorch or rapid drying.

The tree's main job right now is to rebuild its roots, so our focus is on providing steady moisture without overdoing it. With the weather turning hot, checking soil moisture under the mulch is your most important task today to prevent both drought stress and soggy soil.

Top priority: Check the soil moisture under the mulch before deciding to water.

Water only if the top 2-3 inches of soil under the mulch feel dry; if still moist, wait.

Give the leaves a quick scan for any new orange powdery spots or unusual wilting.

Consider shielding the pot's sides from the hottest afternoon sun to keep roots cooler.

A wooden skewer pushed into the soil can give you a clearer, less messy moisture reading than your finger.
