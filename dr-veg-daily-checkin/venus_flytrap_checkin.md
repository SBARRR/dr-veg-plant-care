Venus Flytrap

Today, keep your Venus Flytrap’s media consistently wet by topping off the skull cachepot reservoir if needed, especially with the warm weather and moderate humidity. Make sure it continues to get those 5–6 hours of bright outdoor light but watch for any signs of heat stress in the afternoon sun.

Top priority: Check the water level in the skull cachepot reservoir and refill with distilled or rainwater if it’s low.

Watering guidance: Keep the tray water shallow but steady—don’t let the media dry out or the tray go completely empty for long.

One useful check: Inspect traps and leaves for any new blackened or dead tissue and gently remove only fully dead traps.

Optional care improvement: If you notice any algae on the media surface, lightly remove it without disturbing the roots.

Optional practical idea: Place the pot where it gets morning sun and some afternoon shade to avoid overheating in the hottest part of the day.
