Southern Live Oak

Today, keep your Southern Live Oak in its sunny outdoor spot and avoid watering unless the soil feels dry in the top 2–3 inches. The brief rain and warm temps mean the soil likely holds enough moisture now, so let it dry out a bit to prevent sogginess while you watch those orange spots carefully.

Check soil moisture at the surface before watering.

Water deeply only if the top 2–3 inches of soil are dry.

Inspect new leaves and shoots for any worsening orange spotting or browning.

Optionally, gently increase airflow around the tree by repositioning the pot if possible to reduce fungal risk.
