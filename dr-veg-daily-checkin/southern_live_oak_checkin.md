Southern Live Oak

Today, keep your Southern Live Oak’s soil evenly moist but not soggy, especially after the recent rain and warm weather. Watch the orange spotting closely and avoid any fertilizer or pruning until you see clear signs of strong new growth.

Top priority/action for today: Check the soil moisture by feeling the top 2–3 inches and only water if it feels dry.

Watering guidance: Water deeply until water drains from the pot holes, then let it drain fully without leaving the pot sitting in water.

One useful check: Inspect the leaves near the top shoot for any increase in orange spots or browning and note if new leaves look healthy.

Optional care improvement: If the orange spots worsen, consider gently increasing airflow around the tree by repositioning it slightly to reduce humidity buildup.

Optional practical idea: Set a simple reminder to check soil moisture every 2–3 days to avoid over- or under-watering during this recovery phase.
